We tried NeuralLink!

In an unexpected turn of events, our very own DEI employee, Roberta Frank, who became paraplegic after a tragic baking accident during the pandemic, is now facing a unique challenge with her recent NeuralLink brain-computer interface. Last month, Roberta was selected to test NeuralLink's latest advancement, which had previously allowed her to re-engage in activities she thought she'd never do again, such as participating in our company’s Friday night dodgeball tournaments and even returning to her love of baking. The interface worked wonders for Roberta, who had previously struggled with limited mobility. Thanks to the innovative chip, she was able to experience a level of independence she hadn’t felt since her accident. But last week, as Roberta was waiting for her coffee, an unexpected update was pushed by NeuralLink’s system. Since then, Roberta has been unable to move. The update, still incomplete, has left her in a state of technological limbo, with the chip requiring a reboot to resume its function. The situation has baffled both Roberta and NeuralLink’s technical team. The chip, which had previously allowed Roberta to move and interact with the world more freely, is now frozen, awaiting a reboot to finish the update process. NeuralLink has assured Roberta that they are working around the clock to resolve the issue, but there is no timeline for when she will regain full control. Meanwhile, Roberta, ever the optimist, remains calm and patient, joking that she’s "just waiting for the next software patch to come through." While frustrating, Roberta’s ordeal highlights both the potential and the risks of cutting-edge brain-computer interface technology, offering an important lesson in the need for reliability and user control in such life-altering advancements.

India, March 31, 2025

New Indian start up offering cheaper AI

A new startup is taking the world by storm—Ask Aunty, an AI-powered platform that mimics the wisdom (and occasional nosiness) of your typical Indian aunty. Designed to provide advice on everything from career choices to love life, Ask Aunty uses a blend of machine learning and decades of auntie gossip to offer surprisingly accurate (or wildly inappropriate) suggestions. "We noticed that every family has that one aunty who always has an opinion, and we thought, why not automate it?" says founder Rajesh Kumar. The AI is trained on thousands of hours of Auntie-isms, including beta, have you thought about getting married? and this hairstyle isn’t for you. Initial feedback has been mixed, with some users thrilled by the blunt advice, while others are questioning if it’s acceptable for AI to tell them to “try doing something with your life.”

China, March 31, 2025

Micronesia’s New Tech Startup Solves Wi-Fi Woes with Solar Power... and a Touch of Chaos

A new startup in Micronesia, IslandNet, is solving the region’s Wi-Fi issues by using solar-powered hotspots to bring high-speed internet to the most remote islands. Inspired by the global push for sustainability, IslandNet skips traditional cables and uses the sun instead. Founder Tasi Lomanu explained, “Our islands are so spread out, we thought, why not just use solar power?”
Early tests haven’t been smooth—cloudy days sometimes knock out the Wi-Fi, and one islander joked, “It’s like trying to predict a Wi-Fi taco—spicy or sweet, who knows?” Despite the setbacks, IslandNet is bringing much-needed connectivity, proving that with a little creativity (and a lot of solar power), tech can thrive even in the most unexpected places.
